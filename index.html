FilmED was a 2-week long workshop where I learned about film technique and equipment by shooting and editing various works including a silent film, action sequence, PSA, spot feature and a music video. I had the opportunity to use a 10-pound, $75000 camera, to produce films that expressed my ideas in just two weeks, 8 hours a day. After the summer film workshop, my favorite quote became these famous words of the greatest outdoor adventurer, Bear Grylls: ‚Äúadapt, improvise, overcome.‚Äù

The fast-paced workspace really challenged me to use my flexibility, creativity, and directing skills. The silent film project pushed the boundaries of my ability to communicate a story with minimal props and no dialogue.

Although we could only stay on school property, because I wanted my film to be in a unique location, I decided the protagonist would be a girl scared of public bathrooms. However, I soon realized the allotted 2 hours will not be enough for such a complicated story, especially with almost no pre-production. So before wasting any time, I changed the plot into just a protagonist accidentally going into the wrong gender bathroom. Finding creative alternatives to fit the requirements did not end there. In addition to changes to the storyline, the shoot location did not fit what the project needed. I wanted the bathroom entrance to be large so that going into those doors felt daunting, but the inside should have multiple stalls. I could not find a bathroom on the entire campus that fit what I wanted to show. The clock was running down, so I decided to use two different bathrooms. The camera was cleverly angled so that the footage could be edited together to look like one location.

Whether I had to adjust a storyline or edit footage, I was dedicated to finding the best way to communicate. Film allowed me to combine visual and auditory aspects to produce something that is uniquely my own.
